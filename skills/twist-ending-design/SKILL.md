# Twist Ending Design

## When To Use
- The user asks for "反转", "plot twist", "twist ending", " surprise ending", "欧亨利式", "神反转".
- The user wants to add a reveal that recontextualizes the story.
- The user needs to evaluate whether a planned twist is fair and effective.

## Input To Gather
- Current plot or premise.
- What the audience currently believes.
- What the truth is.
- Genre and tone.
- Desired emotional effect (shock, heartbreak, awe, irony, dread).
- How much the protagonist should know vs. the audience.
- Whether the twist should change the meaning of earlier scenes.

## Design Rules
- A good twist is surprising in the moment but inevitable in retrospect.
- Plant clues early; they should be visible but not obvious.
- The twist must recontextualize character motives or story meaning.
- Avoid deus ex machina; the reveal must arise from established elements.
- Consider the emotional cost of the twist on characters.
- Decide if the twist is a reversal (event changes), revelation (truth exposed), or recognition (identity revealed).
