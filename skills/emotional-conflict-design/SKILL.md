# Emotional Conflict Design

## When To Use
- The user asks for "情感冲突", "感情线", "人物关系", "romance arc", "family drama", "内心矛盾", "character wound", "关系张力".
- The user wants to add emotional depth to a plot-driven script.
- The user needs romantic, familial, or friendship arcs that feel authentic.

## Input To Gather
- Relationship type (romantic, familial, friendship, rivalry, mentor-student).
- Characters involved and their emotional wounds.
- What brings them together and what drives them apart.
- External obstacles (society, war, distance, duty) and internal obstacles (fear, shame, misalignment).
- Desired arc direction (get together, break apart, reconciliation, tragic separation).
- Theme the relationship should express.

## Design Rules
- Emotional conflict must be two-sided; both characters must have valid reasons.
- Use "I love you but..." structures to create wanting-without-having.
- Escalate intimacy and distance in alternating beats.
- Show vulnerability before connection; defenses must lower.
- Avoid generic misunderstandings; conflicts should reveal character.
- The climax of the relationship should mirror the climax of the plot.
