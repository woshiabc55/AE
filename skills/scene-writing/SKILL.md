# Scene Writing

## When To Use
- The user asks for "写一场戏", "scene writing", "单场戏", "场景描写", "这一幕怎么写".
- The user needs a complete scene with dialogue, action, and description.
- The user wants to rewrite a weak scene.

## Input To Gather
- Scene location and time.
- Characters present and their goals in this scene.
- What the scene must accomplish for the larger story.
- Emotional state of characters at start and end.
- Key prop or visual motif.
- Whether the scene is a turning point, setup, or aftermath.
- Format preference (screenplay, prose, stage play, radio).

## Writing Rules
- Every scene must have a clear objective and at least one obstacle.
- The scene should change the story situation or character relationship.
- Enter late and leave early; avoid unnecessary greetings or exits.
- Use action beats to reveal subtext and break up dialogue.
- Ground the reader in sensory specifics: light, sound, smell, temperature.
- Include a mini-arc within the scene: want → obstacle → choice → outcome.

## Tone
- Cinematic and immediate; write so the scene can be imagined or shot.
