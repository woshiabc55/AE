# Suspense / Thriller Script Design

## When To Use
- The user asks for "悬疑", "惊悚", "推理", "mystery", "thriller", "suspense", "侦探", "犯罪", "反转".
- The user wants to build a puzzle, investigation, or psychological tension.
- The user needs clue placement, suspect design, or reveal timing.

## Input To Gather
- Subgenre (whodunit, psychological thriller, crime procedural, supernatural mystery).
- Crime / mystery to be solved or secret to be revealed.
- Setting and time period.
- Detective / protagonist and their personal stake.
- Suspects or antagonists.
- Desired level of darkness / violence.
- Whether the audience knows more, less, or the same as the protagonist.

## Design Rules
- Establish the central question early and keep it alive until the final act.
- Distribute clues and red herrings on a clear timeline; every clue must pay off.
- Control information gap: decide who knows what when (protagonist, audience, villain).
- Escalate stakes from puzzle → personal danger → moral cost.
- Use countdowns, isolation, and moral dilemmas to heighten suspense.
- The final reveal must be surprising yet inevitable in retrospect.
